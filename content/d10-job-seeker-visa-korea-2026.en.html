D-10-1 Job Seeker Status After Graduation: Points-Exemption Requirements (2026)

Updated 2026.01Reading time approx. 7 min

If you are an international student approaching graduation without a confirmed job, the immediate question is whether you can change from study (D-2) to job seeker (D-10-1) status before your stay expires. This article covers the points-system exemption for new graduates, the internship and part-time work rules under D-10-1, and the stay periods and extension review.

"Do I need 60 points for a D-10 visa?" An ordinary job seeker (D-10-1) application is reviewed under a points system combining age, education and Korean-language ability, generally requiring 60 points or more. However, a graduate of a regular Korean university applying for the first time within one year of graduation is exempt from the points review and is assessed centrally on the fact of graduation.

The points system and the new-graduate exemption

The new-graduate exemption applies where all of the following are met: 1) graduation from a regular Korean university (associate degree or higher), 2) a first-time D-10 application, 3) application within one year of graduation, and 4) current stay under study (D-2) status (as of 2026).

The benefit goes beyond skipping the points calculation. The proof of financial capacity required in ordinary applications is not required of exempt applicants — a practical difference for graduates who cannot easily hold funds in an account immediately after finishing their studies.

Meanwhile, besides new graduates, separate exemption and preferential categories exist for graduates with strong Korean-language ability and for holders of advanced degrees from leading universities, so it is worth confirming at the subcategory level which type applies to you.

Internship and part-time work rules

Internships (field training) are permitted during D-10-1 stay, with one restriction: no more than one cumulative year at any single company. There is no limit on total internship time across different companies and no minimum period per company, so internships can continue across several companies as long as no single company exceeds one year. It should be noted, however, that an internship must be reported within 15 days of its start date, and an omitted report can weigh against you in later reviews.

Part-time work is permitted only where all of the following are met: 1) less than three years since graduation, 2) TOPIK level 4 or higher, or Korea Immigration and Integration Program (KIIP) stage 4 or higher, and 3) no past E-1 to E-7 status history (as of 2026). Where these are met, up to 25 hours per week is allowed, or up to 30 hours with TOPIK level 5 or KIIP stage 5. The permitted and restricted industries are the same as for study (D-2) part-time work; manufacturing and construction are among the restricted industries.

Stay periods and the extension review

The period granted per issuance and the maximum total stay differ by type (as of 2026).

The exemption applies to the first application; at extension, the points system applies and 60 points or more are required. With 60–79 points, total stay is limited to one year; with 80 points or more, up to three years. If you plan a longer job search, it is appropriate to aim at securing 80 points.

The extension review also examines your actual job-seeking activity alongside language ability and finances, so records of internships, interviews and applications, and any completed training, should be kept organized during the stay.

When the exemption window is closing

Suppose, for example, an application being considered close to one year after graduation, by a person with a past E-2 status history. Which type governs the review cannot be judged on general criteria alone: the timing of the exemption requirements and the past status history bear in different directions on the grant period and on part-time work eligibility.

In such matters, 1) the interval between graduation and application, 2) the type and duration of past statuses, 3) the expected score if the points system applies, and 4) the subsequent employment and extension plan are examined together, and the advantageous application type and timing can differ accordingly, so case-by-case review is required.

The order of preparation around graduation

Taking the above together, when graduation is confirmed it is advisable first to check whether the exemption requirements are met and the window for applying; to apply for the change before your study (D-2) status expires; and during the stay, to observe the internship reporting duty while keeping records of job-seeking activity in preparation for the extension review.

Frequently asked questions

If I qualify for the exemption, is approval guaranteed?
No. The exemption concerns the application requirements — it removes the points review — while approval itself is a discretionary judgment following review of matters such as your stay record.
Can I work part-time freely on D-10?
No. Part-time work is allowed only where all requirements are met — under three years since graduation, TOPIK 4 or KIIP stage 4 or higher, and no past E-1 to E-7 history — within 25 hours per week (30 with TOPIK 5 or KIIP stage 5).
