D-10 Job Seeker Visa Korea 2026 — The Complete Guide for New Graduates (Points Exemption)

Published 2026.01.16Updated 2026.01.19Read ~9 min

If you have just graduated from a Korean university, the Points System Exemption lets you switch to a D-10 (Job Seeker) status without the 60-point calculation and without proof of bank balance. This guide explains who qualifies, the documents, internship and part-time rules, stay periods, and extensions.

Overview

"I just graduated. Do I really need 60 points for a D-10 visa?"

This is the most common worry for international students in Korea as graduation approaches. The standard D-10-1 Job Seeker Visa usually operates on a strictly calculated points system, requiring age, education, and Korean-language scores to total at least 60 points.

✅ Great newsIf you have just graduated from a university in South Korea, you are exempt from the points system.

At Lawyeon Visa & Immigration Center, we specialize in helping fresh graduates transition smoothly to Job Seeker status without the stress of complex calculations.

The "new graduate" privilege

According to the latest Immigration Office manual, you qualify for the Points System Exemption if you meet these conditions:

Points exemption criteria
CriteriaDetails
TargetInternational students who have graduated from a regular university in South Korea (associate degree or higher)
Conditions1. Applying for a D-10 visa for the first time · 2. Within 1 year after graduation · 3. Currently holding a D-2 student visa
If you fit this description, you do not need to calculate points — you only need to prove you graduated.

Why this is a game changer

The biggest benefit is not just skipping the math. It is about the money.

Standard D-10 applicants must provide a bank statement showing proof of living expenses (approx. 4–5 million KRW). As a new graduate under this exemption rule, however:

ℹ️ No bank balance requiredProof of living expenses (financial ability) is exempt. You do not need to freeze money in your bank account to apply.

Required documents (simplified for graduates)

Since you skip the points evaluation, the document list is much simpler. We handle the preparation for you, but here is what is needed:

Common documents

  • Application form (Form 34)
  • Passport
  • Recent photo
  • Alien Registration Card (ARC)
  • Application fee (100,000 KRW)

Graduate-specific documents

  • Job-seeking plan: a simple statement of how you will look for a job (we can guide you on this)
  • Diploma / degree certificate: usually NOT required — the immigration office can verify through their system
  • Proof of residence: housing contract (lease agreement) or residence certificate
✅ Key pointNo bank balance certificate is required for this specific category.

Internship rules you must know

You can do internships on a D-10 visa, but there are specific rules.

Time limits (2026 regulations)

  • Maximum 1 year cumulative at each individual company.

There is no limit on total internship time across different companies during your entire D-10 period, and no minimum period requirement — you can work at a company for any duration as long as you do not exceed 1 year at that specific company.

✅ Allowed• Company A: 1 year + Company B: 1 year = OK
• A: 6 months + B: 6 months + C: 1 year = OK
• A: 1 year + B: 1 year + C: 1 year = OK
• A: 11 months + B: 1 month + A again: 1 month = OK (A total: 1 year)
🚨 Not allowed• Company A: 1 year 2 months = exceeds 1 year at the same company
• A: 7 months, then later A again for 6 months = A cumulative 13 months

Reporting requirement

⚠️ ReportingYou must report your internship to the immigration office within 15 days of starting. Not reporting can cause serious problems later.

What to submit when reporting:

  • Change of registration form
  • Your internship contract
  • Company registration documents
  • Company's employment insurance certificate

Part-time work: not as simple as you think

Many students assume they can work part-time freely on a D-10 visa. This is not true. You can work part-time only if you meet all of the conditions below.

For 25 hours per week

  • Less than 3 years since you graduated
  • Korean proficiency: TOPIK Level 4+ OR Social Integration Program (KIIP) Level 4+ (passed the intermediate evaluation)
  • No previous E-1 to E-7 visa history (including teaching visas such as E-2, professional visas E-5, E-7, etc.)

For 30 hours per week

With TOPIK Level 5+ OR KIIP Level 5 you can work up to 30 hours per week — but you must still meet all the requirements above.

What jobs are allowed?

Part-time work for D-10 holders follows the same rules as for international students on D-2 visas.

🚨 Restricted industriesManufacturing · Construction · English kids cafés · other restricted businesses. If you do not meet the requirements, you cannot work part-time legally.

How long can you stay?

Extension details

For new graduates (points exempt on first application)

When you apply for extension, you must meet the points system requirements:

  • 60+ points required for extension
  • 60–79 points: each extension is 6 months — maximum 1 year total stay
  • 80+ points: each extension is 1 year — maximum 3 years total stay

Required documents for extension

  • Korean-language certificate (TOPIK or KIIP)
  • Bank statements (proof of financial ability)
  • Proof of active job hunting (internship records, interview records, job applications)
  • Any certificates or training you completed
⚠️ TimingStart preparing for extension at least 1 month before your visa expires.

Other exemption cases

While the "new graduate" case is the most common, the points system is also exempted if you fall into these categories:

High Korean proficiency

  • TOPIK Level 4+ OR KIIP Level 4+ (passed intermediate evaluation)
  • Within 3 years of graduation
  • Stay: 1 year per issuance, up to 3 years total

Promising talent (D-10-T)

  • Graduated from a Times Higher Education or QS World University Rankings Top 200 university
  • Master's degree or higher · within 3 years of graduation
  • Stay: 1 year per issuance, up to 3 years total

Experienced professionals (former E-1 to E-7 holders)

  • Previous experience in E-1 through E-7 professional visa categories
  • Stay: 6 months per issuance, maximum 1 year total

Frequently asked questions

Q. Can I apply for D-10 right after graduation?
Yes. Just make sure to apply within 1 year of graduation to get the points-system exemption.
Q. Do I need TOPIK to get D-10?
No. However, you do need TOPIK 4+ (or KIIP Level 4+) if you want to work part-time.
Q. Do I need to show bank statements?
No — not for your first D-10 application if you are a new graduate within 1 year of graduation.
Q. Can I work part-time immediately after getting D-10?
Only if you meet all the requirements: less than 3 years since graduation, TOPIK 4+ or KIIP 4+, and no previous E-1~E-7 visa history.
Q. I have TOPIK 3. Can I work part-time?
No. You need at least TOPIK Level 4 or KIIP Level 4 to be eligible.
Q. Do I have to report my internship?
Yes — within 15 days of starting. Not reporting can cause problems later.
Q. Can I intern at multiple companies for more than 1 year total?
Yes. There is no limit on total internship time. The only limit is that you cannot work at any single company for more than 1 year cumulative.
Q. Is there a minimum internship period at each company?
No. You can intern for any duration up to 1 year at each company.
Q. I worked at Company A for 8 months. Can I go back later?
Yes, but your cumulative time at Company A cannot exceed 1 year — so you can work 4 more months maximum there.
Q. How long does the application take?
Usually 2–4 weeks. It may take longer if your documents are incomplete.
Q. What if more than 1 year has passed since I graduated?
You can still apply, but you lose the points exemption and must meet the 60+ points requirement.
Q. If I get 60–79 points for extension, how long can I stay?
You get 6 months per extension, with a maximum total stay of 1 year.
Q. I used to have an E-2 teaching visa. Can I work part-time on D-10?
No. Any prior E-1 through E-7 visa (including E-2) makes you ineligible for the part-time allowance on D-10.
Q. What if I did not graduate from a university in Korea?
You will likely need the points system, or qualify under the "world top university" exemption (Times/QS Top 200).
Q. What part-time jobs can I do with D-10?
The same jobs as international students on D-2. Manufacturing, construction, and English kids cafés are among the restricted industries.
Q. I completed a caregiver training program. What are my stay limits?
6 months per issuance, maximum total stay 3 years.
Q. I have previous E-7 experience. What are my D-10 limits?
6 months per issuance, maximum total stay 1 year (not 3 years).

Common mistakes to avoid

1
Applying after D-2 expires
Apply before your D-2 visa runs out.
2
Not reporting internships
Report within 15 days of starting any internship.
3
Assuming a total internship-time limit
There is no total limit — just do not exceed 1 year at any single company.
4
Working part-time without qualifications
Meet all requirements: TOPIK 4+, less than 3 years since graduation, no previous E visa.
5
Not preparing for extension early
Gather documents for 60+ points at least 1 month before expiry.
6
Not keeping job-hunting records
Keep proof of all applications, interviews, and training — you will need these for extension.
7
Thinking 60–79 points allows 3 years
60–79 points allows only 1 year total. To stay longer, you need 80+ points.

Important tips

Before you graduate

  • Find out when you can get your graduation certificate
  • Make sure you have a rental contract
  • Write a simple job-search plan
  • Check when your D-2 visa expires
  • Get TOPIK 4+ if you plan to work part-time

While you have D-10

  • Report any internship within 15 days of starting
  • Track your internship time at each company (do not exceed 1 year per company)
  • Keep records of all your job-search activities
  • Aim for 80+ points if you want to stay for 3 years

For extension

  • Start preparing 1 month before your visa expires
  • Collect documents to meet the 60+ points requirement
  • Get a TOPIK or KIIP certificate to boost your points
  • Critical: aim for 80+ points to get 1-year extensions instead of 6-month ones
