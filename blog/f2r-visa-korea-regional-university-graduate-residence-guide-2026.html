F-2-R Visa Korea 2026: Complete Residence Visa Guide for Regional University Graduates

üìÖ 2026.02.06
F-2-R Regional Residence Visa Application Apply Now ‚Üí

Graduation is approaching. Your D-2 visa is about to expire. Most international students aim for an E-7 (work visa).

But if you graduated from a regional university, there may be a far better option than E-7.

The F-2-R visa (Regional Talent Residence Visa) is a residence visa granted to foreign nationals willing to settle in depopulating areas of South Korea. You can sponsor your family, work in any industry within the region after the first year, and open a clear path to permanent residency (F-5).

A total of 85 local governments are participating, with a combined quota of 5,072 slots. Most graduates do not even know this visa exists.

What Is the F-2-R Visa?

The F-2-R is a visa created by the Korean government to combat rural depopulation. When a local government in a designated depopulating area recommends a foreign national who agrees to settle there, the Ministry of Justice grants F-2 (residence) status.

The differences from an E-7 visa are significant:

Key Point: The F-2-R ties you to a region, not an employer. With an E-7, every time you change jobs, the new employer must submit an employment justification statement and your eligibility is re-assessed. With the F-2-R, once your first year is complete, you can work at any employer in any industry within your designated region.

Can D-2 Students Apply Directly?

Yes. You can switch from D-2 directly to F-2-R without going through D-10 first.

The following visa statuses are excluded from F-2-R eligibility:

üö®
Ineligible statuses: D-3 (Industrial Training), D-4 (General Training), E-6-2 (Hotel Entertainment), E-8 (Seasonal Work), E-9 (Non-professional Employment), E-10 (Crew), G-1 (Miscellaneous), H-1 (Working Holiday)

D-2 (Student) is NOT on this exclusion list.

Prospective graduates can also apply. If you can prove that you are expected to graduate within 6 months from the application date (with a certificate of expected graduation signed by the university president or department head), you are eligible. You must submit your actual degree certificate when you first apply for a stay extension.

You must satisfy both the Ministry of Justice's baseline requirements and the individual criteria set by each local government.

Income:

  • Only your own income counts

3. Residence Requirements

  • You must actually reside in the area that recommended you
  • First 2 years: You must live in the recommending municipality
  • After 2 years: You may move to another depopulating area within the same province
  • Total residence restriction period: 5 years

4. Employment or Business (Meet One)

Employment:

  • Directly employed by a company located in the recommending area
  • Salary: 70% or more of GNI (‚âà ‚Ç©2,914,000/month or more)
  • Contract term of 1 year or more; work must begin within 3 months of application
  • Direct employment only ‚Äî dispatch, staffing agencies, and daily-wage contracts are NOT accepted
  • For the first year, you cannot change workplaces (exceptions: business closure, human rights violations ‚Äî requires re-recommendation by local government)
  • Any industry except restricted fields

  • Business premises must be within the recommending area

During your permitted stay, you must maintain employment or business for at least 3/4 of the period (9 months per year). You may also switch between employment and business within the recommending area.

5. Korean Language Proficiency

You must meet one of the following:

  • TOPIK Level 4 or higher, or
  • KIIP (Social Integration Program) Stage 4 or higher completion (or assigned to Stage 5+ via pre-assessment)

Family Sponsorship

This is one of the biggest advantages of the F-2-R.

Who you can sponsor: Spouse + unmarried minor children ‚Üí They receive F-3-1R status.

Spouse requirements:

  • Must live at the same address as the F-2-R holder (independent residential unit)
  • Must complete KIIP Stage 2 or higher within 2 years of status change (maximum 6-month extension if incomplete)
  • Can work within the recommending area (requires Permission for Activities Outside of Status)

Minor children requirements:

  • School-age children must be enrolled in school (elementary/middle/high)
  • Violation may result in cancellation of the primary holder's (F-2-R) visa

Common Mistakes

üö®
Mistake #1: Applying to any depopulating area
Not all 107 areas participate. Only 85 municipalities with allocated quotas are eligible, and each has different selection criteria.
üö®
Mistake #2: Applying with a dispatch or staffing agency contract
Only direct employment is accepted. Dispatch, staffing agency, and daily-wage contracts will be rejected.
üö®
Mistake #3: Not completing address registration within 30 days
If you used the grace period provision, you MUST complete address registration within 30 days of approval. You must also register within 15 days of the lease contract date.
üö®
Mistake #4: Changing jobs within the first year
Only allowed in cases of business closure or human rights violations, and only with re-recommendation from the local government. Unauthorized job changes are grounds for visa cancellation.
üö®
Mistake #5: Confusing criminal fines with immigration penalties
Different disqualification standards apply to each. If you have any prior dispositions, get a professional assessment.
üö®
Mistake #6: Preparing with TOPIK Level 3
The requirement was raised to Level 4 starting in 2025. The old standard no longer applies.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I apply for F-2-R while on D-10 status?
A: Yes, as long as your previous status before D-10 was not on the exclusion list (D-3, D-4, E-6-2, etc.). If your path was D-2 ‚Üí D-10, there is no problem.

Q: What if I don't have TOPIK Level 4?
A: You cannot apply. TOPIK Level 4 or KIIP Stage 4 completion is mandatory. TOPIK is administered 6 times per year.

Q: Can I move to Seoul after 2 years?
A: No. You may only move to another depopulating area within the same province. The residence restriction lasts a total of 5 years.

Q: Can F-2-R lead to permanent residency (F-5)?
A: Time spent on F-2 status counts toward F-5 eligibility requirements. The specific path depends on your individual circumstances.

Q: I'm graduating in August. When should I start preparing?
A: Now. Confirming a participating municipality ‚Üí preparing documents ‚Üí obtaining the local government recommendation ‚Üí filing with immigration takes at least 2‚Äì3 months.

Q: Can my spouse work?
A: Yes, with a Permission for Activities Outside of Status. They can work in any non-restricted industry within the recommending area.

Q: What is the minimum salary requirement?
A: 70% of GNI = approximately ‚Ç©34,970,000/year (‚âà ‚Ç©2,914,000/month) (applicable Apr 2025 ‚Äì Mar 2026).

Q: I have a past immigration penalty. Is it disqualifying?
A: Penalties totaling ‚Ç©5 million or more within the last 3 years, or 3 or more penalty dispositions within 3 years, are disqualifying. Criminal fines and immigration penalties are assessed separately.
